Why I will travel to Ghana:

Ghana is geographically located in the center of the world on today's world map. It is located on the west coast of the continent of Africa, boarded by the Atlantic ocean, with 0 degrees latitude located on the equator. It has lots of sunny beaches, cool waters, and great traditional street food. The capital is port city Accra, commercial hub, most populous city and home of the nation's oldest university. It is the birth place of Kwame Nkrumah, first Prime Minister and President of Ghana who lead the country to independence from Great Britain in 1957. It is also the home of the slave port 'Door of No Return' where millions of Africans where shipped as slaves to the Americas, Brazil, and the Caribbean.

Why I will travel to Kemet:

Kemet is an ancient African civilization established by Nesut(King) Narmer after uniting the upper and lower Nile Valley Hapi regions with ancient Nubia in 3150 BCE (before common era), creating the standard for modern civilization, religion, science, and culture of moral living. The Nile (formally knows as the Hapi River) is 4,132 miles long and was the bread basket of fertile soil within the land proving natural menials within the land's surface resulting in dark colored soil consisting of the ancient name Kement or Ta Merry meaning black land that contained plentiful agricultural resources for the region. The population ranges from 6 million to 1.5 million over a a three thousand year period. It was the birthplace of the world's first universities including Luxor and Carnak where philosophers such as Pythagoras, Aristotle, Socrates, Plato, and hundreds of other ancient scholars were educated.

Why I will travel to Sierra Leone:

Sierra Leone's meaning of its name translates to Mountains of Lions. The capital city is Freetown. Freetown is the primary point for commercial trade and is known for its white sands, beaches, and resorts. Sierra Leon established freedom in 1960, in which Freetown is a location established by former slaves who gathered and established their own city. however there were several wars within the country subsidized by outside influences up until 2007. Sierra Leone's primary exports are diamonds, coffee, fish, crude oil, and cocoa. Its primary imports are industrial machinery, transportation equipment, and refined fossil fuels.

Why I will travel to the Bahamas:

The Bahamas are achipelago islands located southeast of the United States and north of Cuba surrounded by the Atlantic ocean. The capital city of this West Indies island is Nassau with a population of 266,100. It is a popular cruse line stop and is located near sever other tourist attraction sites including neighboring Paradise Island. It is popular for beaches uses for snorkeling and tropical weather conditions. The Bahamas fully gained independence in 1973 under the leadership of Sir Lynden Pindling of the Progressive Liberal Party.
